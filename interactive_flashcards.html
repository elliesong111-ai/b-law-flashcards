Q3

Sovereignty A sovereign state is self-governing and independent, and its authority is internationally recognized. A government has power to command control over the population and over a specific geographic region.

Answer

Sovereign states typically have: Identifiable symbols like flags and a national anthem Some uniform law Common currency Postal system A defined population

Q4

Foreign Sovereign Immunities Act (FSIA) The Foreign Sovereign Immunities Act is a U.S. federal statute providing that a foreign government’s public sector acts fall outside the scope of a U.S. court’s jurisdiction. It applies only when a foreign government is the defendant.

Answer

Exceptions to the FSIA: The country waived its immunity. The country engaged in commercial activity. The country violated property rights in violation of international law.

Q5

Comity The doctrine of comity states that, in some circumstances, nations will recognize and defer to the legislative, executive, or judicial acts (including court decisions) of other countries.

Answer

Comity requires that a U.S. court abstain from exercising jurisdiction over a dispute if a foreign court is in a better position to adjudicate it or if it is more appropriate to apply foreign law than U.S. law. U.S. courts recognize foreign judicial decisions as long as: The foreign court had jurisdiction; The foreign proceedings have been “orderly, fair and not detrimental to the nation’s interests” (Basic v. Fitzroy Engineering, Ltd. 949 F. Supp. 1333 (ND Ill 1996) aff’d 132 F. 3d 36 (7th Cir 1997); and Recognizing the decision will not violate U.S. laws or U.S. public policy.

Q7

Treaties and Conventions A treaty is “an international agreement concluded between States in written form and governed by international law, whether embodied in a single instrument or in two or more related instruments.”

Answer

Treaties can be bilateral or multilateral. Under U.S. law, the executive branch negotiates treaties. The U.S. Senate provides advice and consent and ratifies it by two-thirds vote. It is then signed by the president, at which point it is binding.

Q10

Tariff and Non-Tariff Barriers to Trade A tariff is a tax levied on imported goods. Tariffs can be calculated as a percentage of the value of the imported goods, on the basis of the number or weight of the imported goods, by the unit, or a combination of these.

Answer

A non-tariff barrier is any type of restriction on imports other than tariffs. For example, quotas are government-imposed restrictions on the number or value of goods that can be imported; and embargoes are government bans on trade with a particular country.

Q11

World Trade Organization (WTO) Principles The WTO is “the only global international organization dealing with the rules of trade between nations” (www.wto.org).

Answer

The WTO aims to liberalize trade by invoking these principles: Normal Trade Relations (NTR). Each member must give equal trade privileges and benefits to all member nations in relation to the same imports. National Treatment. Each member must apply the same standards to imports that are applied to domestic goods. Elimination of trade barriers. Nontariff barriers are prohibited.

Q12

Dumping and Subsidies Dumping occurs when products of one country are introduced into the commerce of another country at less than the normal value of the product (GATT Article 6(1)). The normal value of the product is the price charged for that product in the exporter’s home market.

Answer

A subsidy is a financial contribution by a government that confers a benefit (Agreement on Subsidies and Countervailing Measures, section 1.1). A specific subsidy is a subsidy available only to a particular enterprise or industry. Some specific subsidies are prohibited subsidies, which are automatically illegal, and some are actionable subsidies, which are illegal if the subsidy has an adverse effect on the complaining country.

Q13

United Nations (UN) The UN preamble states that its founders wanted “to save succeeding generations from the scourge of war,” and the charter authorizes “collective measures for the prevention and removal of threats to peace, and for the suppression of acts of aggression or other breaches of the peace.”

Answer

The UN is comprised of five active bodies: the General Assembly, Security Council, Secretariat, International Court of Justice, and Economic and Social Council.

Q16

World Intellectual Property Organization (WIPO) The mission of the World Intellectual Property Organization (WIPO) is “to promote innovation and creativity for the economic, social and cultural development of all countries, through a balanced and effective international intellectual property system” (www.wipo.org).

Answer

Some of WIPO’s responsibilities are: Arbitrate disputes relating to patents, trademarks, copyrights and internet domain names Administer intellectual property treaties and conventions Administer systems and build networks to facilitate sharing knowledge, transferring technology, and simplifying intellectual property transactions
